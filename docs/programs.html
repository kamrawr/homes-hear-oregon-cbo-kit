Program Type: Performance-based whole-home energy efficiency retrofits

What HOMES Covers

HOMES provides rebates for comprehensive home energy efficiency improvements that achieve significant, modeled energy savings. This is NOT a measure-by-measure program‚Äîit rewards overall performance improvement.

Key Requirements

  • Modeled Savings: Energy savings must be calculated using DOE-approved modeling software (e.g., Home Energy Score, HERS-based tools)
  • Baseline Data: Requires 12 months of utility usage data before retrofit
  • Quality Assurance: Work must be performed by qualified contractors meeting state standards
  • Comprehensive Approach: Whole-home assessment required; may include insulation, air sealing, HVAC upgrades, windows, doors
  • Project Cap: Rebate cannot exceed 50% of total project cost for moderate-income; up to 80% for low-income

Retroactivity

‚úì HOMES CAN be retroactive to projects completed after August 16, 2022, if all program requirements are met (including modeling and documentation).

Eligible Measures (Examples)

Any combination of measures that achieves required savings thresholds:

  • Attic, wall, floor, and foundation insulation
  • Air sealing and duct sealing
  • High-efficiency HVAC systems (heating and cooling)
  • High-efficiency water heaters
  • Energy-efficient windows and doors
  • Smart thermostats and controls

Program Type: Point-of-sale rebates for qualifying electric appliances and equipment

What HEAR Covers

HEAR provides instant discounts on qualifying electric appliances, heat pumps, and necessary electrical infrastructure upgrades. Rebates are typically applied at the point of sale or through participating contractors.

Key Requirements

  • ENERGY STAR Certification: Most appliances must meet ENERGY STAR or equivalent efficiency standards
  • Income Verification: Required for all applicants; streamlined processes available through implementers
  • One Rebate Per Category: Households can receive one rebate per appliance type (e.g., can't get two heat pump rebates)
  • Necessary Upgrades: Electrical panel and wiring upgrades are only eligible if needed to support qualifying appliances
  • Quality Installation: Work must be performed by licensed, qualified contractors

Retroactivity

‚úó HEAR CANNOT be retroactive. Only purchases and installations made after the official program launch date will qualify.

Additional Details

  • Point-of-Sale Discount: Rebates are typically applied as instant discounts at participating retailers or through contractors
  • Stacking with Other Incentives: May be combined with utility rebates and tax credits, but check specific program rules
  • Multifamily: Special considerations and potentially higher caps for multifamily properties

Program Implementation in Oregon

Oregon's Allocation

  • Total Funding: $113.6 million across both programs
  • Estimated Direct Rebates: ~$91.4 million to households after administrative costs
  • Expected Launch: 2026 (pending final DOE approval)

Roles & Responsibilities

Oregon Department of Energy (ODOE)

State program oversight, federal reporting, policy guidance, and equity priorities

Energy Trust & Earth Advantage

Day-to-day program implementation: rebate processing, income verification, contractor management, CBO partnerships

Utilities

Customer outreach, bill payment data, complementary incentives, data sharing with implementers

CBOs

Community outreach, intake support, application assistance, cultural and linguistic access, equity focus

Contractors

Perform work, meet quality standards, process point-of-sale rebates, customer education

Justice40 & Equity Considerations

Both programs prioritize disadvantaged communities and aim to meet Justice40 goals (40% of benefits to these communities). Oregon's design includes:

  • Higher rebate amounts for low-income households
  • Targeted outreach to environmental justice communities
  • Partnerships with trusted CBOs serving priority populations
  • Streamlined income verification processes
  • Multilingual and culturally-responsive program materials
