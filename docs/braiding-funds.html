Fund Braiding Guide

Rules and strategies for combining multiple funding sources with HOMES and HEAR

Last Updated: Based on public guidance as of November 5, 2025. This is a working reference, not legal advice.

Purpose and Audience

This guide is for Community-Based Organizations (CBOs) and program staff in Oregon helping households access HOMES and HEAR programs while stacking multiple funding sources for whole-home retrofit and repair projects.

1. Quick Glossary

Term Definition
HOMES Home Efficiency Rebates (IRA §50121). Performance-based rebates for whole-home efficiency retrofits measured by percent energy savings.
HEAR Home Electrification and Appliance Rebates (IRA §50122). Point-of-sale rebates for high-efficiency electric equipment for LMI households only.
LMI Low- or Moderate-Income: <80% AMI (low-income) or 80-150% AMI (moderate-income)
AMI Area Median Income - income thresholds vary by county and household size
QEP Qualified Electrification Project - bundle of HEAR-eligible upgrades in one project
WAP Weatherization Assistance Program - federal weatherization grants for low-income households

Income Tiers

Tier AMI Range HOMES HEAR

Moderate-Income 80-150% AMI ✓ Eligible ✓ Eligible (up to 50% of cost)

Building Types

  • Single-family homes (owner-occupied and rental)
  • Multifamily buildings (5+ units)
  • Manufactured homes
  • 1-4 unit properties

Funding Set-Asides

HOMES: At least 40% reserved for low-income households, plus 10% for low-income multifamily
HEAR: 60% for low-income single-family, plus 20% for low-income multifamily

4. Federal Fund-Braiding Rules

⚠️ Critical: Two Key Prohibitions

All HOMES and HEAR projects must obey BOTH of these federal rules:

❌ Prohibition 1: Cannot Exceed Project Cost

Total upfront funding (all federal grants + federal loans + non-federal funding) cannot exceed 100% of project cost.

❌ Prohibition 2: No Double-Dipping Federal Grants

Neither HOMES nor HEAR may be combined with any other federal grant or rebate for the same single upgrade.

Same upgrade = that specific heat pump, water heater, panel, wiring segment, insulation job, etc.

If using multiple federal grants in one project, they must fund distinct, separable upgrades with clear invoicing.

Allowed Combinations

✓ HOMES + HEAR (Both IRA Rebates)

Projects may receive rebates from both programs, but not for the same upgrade.

Example: Use HOMES for whole-home retrofit package (envelope + HVAC) and HEAR for distinct electrification (panel, stove).

✓ Other Federal Grants (WAP, DOE grants, etc.)

Can combine WAP + HOMES/HEAR on the same house, but each funding stream must pay for different upgrades.

Example: WAP covers health & safety + basic air sealing; HOMES funds deep efficiency package; HEAR funds panel + HPWH.

✓ Federal Loans or GGRF Financing

Federal loan programs are not treated as grants and can finance remaining costs beyond IRA rebates.

Must still respect the 100% project cost ceiling.

✓ Non-Federal Funding

State, local, utility, and philanthropic funds can be layered with HOMES/HEAR to cover remaining costs.

Must respect 100% cost ceiling and any restrictions from those programs.

✓ Tax Credits (Limited Timeframe)

Can combine tax credits with rebates under specific rules. Congress terminated IRA efficiency tax credits early - only available through December 31, 2025.

5. HEAR-Specific Rules

Key Constraints

  • HEAR rebate paid only for projects initiated after state launch authorization (no pre-launch work)
  • Each dwelling receives rebate only once per appliance category
  • Only one rebate for: electric stove, cooktop, range, OR oven (pick one)
  • Can receive both kitchen appliance AND heat pump dryer, but combined max is $840
  • Maximum per dwelling: $14,000 total
CBO Strategy: HEAR rebates are essentially once-per-category per dwelling. Don't "burn" a category on a marginal upgrade if a deeper project is coming. Coordinate carefully for multifamily buildings.

6. Oregon Program Design Details

Relationship to Other Oregon Programs

ODOE encourages combining HOMES/HEAR with existing incentives:

  • Energy Trust and public utility efficiency programs
  • State heat pump programs:
    • Oregon Heat Pump Purchase Program
    • Community Heat Pump Deployment Program
    • Oregon Rental Home Heat Pump Program
  • OHCS Weatherization Assistance Program (WAP)
  • Use Energy HIPPO to find all available incentives

Contractor Requirements

Federal rules require most HOMES and HEAR projects to use pre-approved qualified contractors.

  • Build relationships early with contractors likely to enroll
  • Ensure contractors understand line-item invoicing requirements
  • Documentation of which fund paid for which upgrade

7. Practical Fund-Braiding Logic

High-Level Intake Process

1. Determine Geography

Identify whether home is in Energy Trust or Earth Advantage territory

2. Assess Income

Confirm AMI tier and screen for categorical eligibility (SNAP, LIHEAP, etc.)

3. Check Safety

Identify deal-breakers for electrification (unsafe wiring, severe issues). May need WAP first.

4. Define Project Type

Whole-home retrofit (HOMES anchor) vs. targeted electrification (HEAR anchor) vs. mixed

5. Inventory Funding

Federal (HOMES, HEAR, WAP), State (heat pump programs), Utility, Local/Philanthropic, Loans

6. Assign Upgrades

One federal grant per upgrade. Use state/utility/philanthropic to close gaps. Total ≤100% cost.

8. Example Stacking Patterns

Note: These are illustrative patterns, not program commitments. Always confirm details with ODOE and implementers.

Pattern A: Low-Income Deep Retrofit + Electrification

Household Profile

<80% AMI, single-family, gas furnace + water heater, high energy burden, significant shell issues

Potential Funding Stack

Funding Source Covers
WAP (Federal) Critical health & safety, baseline weatherization (air sealing/insulation different from HOMES/HEAR scope)
HOMES (Federal) Whole-home efficiency package (remaining insulation, duct sealing, smart thermostat, potentially heat pump if not using HEAR). Must achieve ≥20% savings.
HEAR (Federal) Distinct upgrades: panel/service upgrade, branch-circuit wiring, heat pump water heater (if HOMES covers space heating)
Utility (Non-Federal) Additional rebates for heat pump and HPWH, stacked on top of federal
GGRF Loan (Federal Loan) Covers remaining customer co-pay with affordable payments

Key: Break scope into clearly separable line items with funding source tags. Decide early whether heat pump is funded by HOMES or HEAR.

Pattern B: Moderate-Income Multifamily

Household Profile

100% AMI renter in 6-unit building, electric resistance heat, high bills

Potential Funding Stack

Funding Source Covers
HEAR (Federal) Per-unit allocations (up to $14k/LMI unit) for central heat pump system + panel/wiring
HOMES (Federal) Separate shell upgrades (windows, added insulation) tied to whole-building savings - must be different measures than HEAR
OHCS WAP (Federal) Different upgrades (health & safety, ventilation) not covered by HOMES/HEAR
Utility/Local (Non-Federal) Additional incentives for central HVAC or shell measures

Key: Track per-unit caps. Ensure no unit exceeds $14k HEAR maximum or receives duplicate federal grants for same upgrade.

Pattern C: >150% AMI Homeowner

Household Profile

>150% AMI, already electrified, wants shell upgrades

Eligibility Reality

  • Not eligible for HEAR (limited to ≤150% AMI)
  • Eligible for HOMES if retrofits meet savings thresholds

Potential Funding Stack

  • HOMES for deep shell work + system tuning (≥20% savings)
  • IRA tax credits if work completed by Dec 31, 2025
  • Energy Trust/utility incentives
  • Private financing

9. Tactics & Strategies

Design Around Upgrades, Not Programs

Core Principle: Start by designing the optimal technical package, then assign each upgrade to the best available funding source.

Invoice & Documentation Requirements

Critical for Compliance:
  • Require line-item invoices with materials, labor, and per-upgrade cost
  • Add column for "Funding Source" (HOMES, HEAR, WAP, Utility, etc.)
  • Maintain funding stack worksheet per project:
    • Total all upfront funding (must be ≤100% of project cost)
    • Confirm no upgrade has more than one federal grant
    • Track HEAR category usage vs. $14k cap
  • Store income documentation and utility territory verification securely

CBO Workflow Best Practices

  • Segment clients by income and housing type
  • Build standard project archetypes for common scenarios:
    • Old gas single-family homes in IOU territory
    • All-electric manufactured homes
    • Small LMI multifamily
    • Tribal housing
  • Pre-define for each archetype:
    • Preferred funding stack
    • Contractor partners
    • Documentation checklist

Using Loans Strategically

Federal loans and GGRF products are separate from federal grants:

  • Use HOMES/HEAR + WAP + state/utility funds to bring cost down as close to zero as possible
  • Use low-interest loans only for remaining co-pays
  • Aim for short terms for low-income households
  • For very low-income with no repayment capacity, prioritize direct grants over loans
  • Use philanthropic dollars to buy down principal/interest

10. Risk Flags & Open Items

⚠️ Implementation Details Still Evolving

Even with published narratives and FAQs, some details will continue to evolve through ODOE's Implementation Blueprints and implementer program manuals.

Watch For:

  • Final Oregon-specific HOMES/HEAR program manuals from ODOE, Energy Trust, and Earth Advantage
  • Updates to tax credit availability and legislative changes
  • New OHCS, PCEF, local, or philanthropic programs that interact with these rebates

When In Doubt:

Golden Rules:

  • ✗ Do NOT stack two federal grants on the same line item
  • ✓ Keep total upfront funding ≤100% of project cost
  • ✓ Maintain full documentation

This keeps you inside the guardrails while maximizing savings and equity for the households you serve.
