Eligibility Guidelines

Income thresholds, housing requirements, and documentation for HOMES and HEAR

Income Eligibility

Both HOMES and HEAR use Area Median Income (AMI) to determine household eligibility and rebate amounts. Income limits vary by county and household size.

Income Categories

Category Income Range HOMES Max Rebate HEAR Max Rebate
Low-Income ≤80% AMI $8,000 $14,000
Moderate-Income 80-150% AMI $4,000 $8,000
Above 150% AMI >150% AMI May qualify for HOMES (check with implementer) Not eligible for HEAR
Finding AMI Thresholds: Specific income limits by county and household size will be published by ODOE and implementers. These are updated annually. Check with Energy Trust or Earth Advantage for current limits in your service area.

Income Verification

Implementers will accept various forms of income documentation:

  • Tax returns: Previous year's federal tax return (1040)
  • Pay stubs: Recent pay stubs covering 30-60 days
  • W-2 or 1099 forms: Previous year's wage statements
  • Benefit letters: SSI, SSDI, SNAP, TANF, Medicaid, public housing assistance
  • Self-declaration: For households with barriers to traditional documentation
  • Automatic enrollment: Households already verified for certain programs (utility assistance, WAP, LIHEAP)
Streamlined Verification: Implementers are developing simplified income verification processes, especially for households already enrolled in other assistance programs.

Housing Eligibility

Eligible Housing Types

Single-Family Homes

  • Owner-occupied
  • Site-built and manufactured homes
  • 1-4 unit properties (if owner-occupied)

Multifamily Buildings

  • 5+ unit properties
  • Special considerations and caps may apply
  • May require property owner participation

Rental Housing

  • Tenant-occupied units may be eligible
  • Requires landlord coordination
  • Check program rules for specific pathways

Ownership Status

Requirements differ between programs and implementers:

  • Owner-occupied: Straightforward eligibility for both HOMES and HEAR
  • Rental properties: May require property owner application or tenant-landlord coordination
  • Mixed-use properties: Residential portions may qualify; check with implementers
Renter Participation: For rental properties, confirm whether the tenant, landlord, or both must participate. Rules are still being finalized—check with Energy Trust or Earth Advantage for updates.

Geographic Eligibility

Oregon Location Requirement

The home must be located in Oregon. All Oregon counties and utility territories are eligible, but you must work with the correct implementer for your service area.

Which Implementer Serves You?

Energy Trust of Oregon

Serves investor-owned utility territories:

  • Portland General Electric (PGE)
  • Pacific Power
  • NW Natural Gas
  • Cascade Natural Gas
  • Avista

Contact: energytrust.org

Earth Advantage

Serves consumer-owned utilities and Idaho Power:

  • Municipal electric utilities
  • Electric cooperatives (co-ops)
  • People's Utility Districts (PUDs)
  • Idaho Power territory in Oregon

Contact: earthadvantage.org

Not sure which utility you have? Check your electric bill or ask the household to identify their electric and gas providers. The implementer assignment is based on the electric utility.

Required Documentation

For All Applicants

  • Proof of residence: Utility bill, lease agreement, property tax statement, or mortgage statement
  • Proof of income: As described above (tax returns, pay stubs, benefit letters, etc.)
  • Household size verification: May require list of household members
  • Photo ID: May be required for primary applicant

For HOMES Projects

  • Utility billing history: 12 months of electric/gas usage data (often obtained directly from utility by implementer)
  • Energy modeling: Pre-retrofit home energy assessment and post-retrofit modeling (performed by qualified contractor/assessor)
  • Contractor estimates: Scope of work and cost estimates
  • Project completion documentation: Invoices, receipts, photos

For HEAR Projects

  • Equipment specifications: Model numbers, ENERGY STAR certification
  • Purchase documentation: Receipts, invoices (may be processed automatically at point of sale)
  • Installation verification: Contractor certification of proper installation
CBO Tip: Help households gather documentation before referring to implementers. Having documents ready speeds up processing and reduces barriers.

Special Considerations

Priority Populations

The programs prioritize households that are:

  • Low-income (≤80% AMI)
  • Located in disadvantaged communities (Justice40 areas)
  • Living in older, inefficient housing stock
  • Currently using fossil fuel heating systems (candidates for electrification)
  • Facing high energy burden (energy costs are >6% of household income)

Households Without Traditional Documentation

Implementers are developing alternative pathways for:

  • Undocumented residents
  • Households without SSNs
  • Cash-based income households
  • Households fleeing domestic violence
Privacy & Safety: Work with implementers to understand what minimum data is required and how to protect vulnerable community members. Self-attestation and categorical eligibility options may be available.

Stacking with Other Programs

HOMES and HEAR can often be combined with:

  • Weatherization Assistance Program (WAP): Federal weatherization program for low-income households
  • LIHEAP: Low Income Home Energy Assistance Program
  • Utility rebates: Existing energy efficiency incentives from utilities
  • Federal tax credits: 25C (energy efficiency) and 25D (renewable energy) tax credits
  • Local programs: County, city, or nonprofit energy assistance programs

Work with implementers to optimize rebate stacking and avoid duplication of benefits for the same measures.

Eligibility Quick Check

✓ Likely Eligible for Both HOMES & HEAR

  • Oregon resident
  • Household income ≤150% AMI
  • Owner-occupied home OR renter with landlord coordination
  • Served by participating utility

✓ Eligible for HOMES Only

  • Income may be above 150% AMI (check implementer rules)
  • Comprehensive retrofit planned
  • Can achieve ≥20% or ≥35% energy savings
  • Has 12 months of utility data

✓ Eligible for HEAR Only

  • Income ≤150% AMI
  • Needs specific appliance replacements
  • Purchases after program launch date
  • May not need whole-home retrofit
Next Step: Once you determine likely eligibility, refer households to the appropriate implementer (Energy Trust or Earth Advantage) for formal intake and verification.
